Weight Units Explained

Precious metals are measured in several units depending on the region and market. Here are the most common ones used in our calculator:

Gram (g)

The standard metric unit. International retail prices are often quoted per gram.

Troy Ounce (oz t)

1 Troy Ounce = 31.1035 grams. This is the official unit for global market spot prices (like on TradingView).

Tola

1 Tola = 11.6638 grams. Widely used in South Asian countries like Pakistan and India.

Avoirdupois Ounce

1 Standard Ounce = 28.3495 grams. Used for common items (food, etc.), but not used in the metal markets.

The Market Standard

When you look at the TradingView chart or global news, the price is almost always shown in USD per Troy Ounce.

Why Troy Ounces? The Troy system is a specific system of weights used for precious metals and gemstones. A Troy ounce is heavier (31.1g) than a regular supermarket ounce (28.3g). To calculate your local price, you must first convert this global Troy Ounce price into grams or Tolas.

Gold vs. Silver

Both Gold and Silver are traded as commodities using the same weight units (Troy Ounces). However, their market behavior differs:

  • Gold: Often viewed as a "safe haven" asset and store of value. It has high density and low industrial reactiveness.
  • Silver: While also a precious metal, it has massive industrial use (electronics, solar panels), making it more sensitive to industrial economic cycles.
